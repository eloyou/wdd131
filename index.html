Camarines Sur, Philippines

Camarines Sur, the largest province in the Bicol Region of the Philippines, is known for its rich natural beauty and cultural heritage. It is home to Mount Isarog, a dormant volcano and biodiversity hotspot, as well as the Caramoan Islands, famous for their stunning beaches and as a filming location for Survivor. Naga City, a religious and cultural center, hosts the annual Peñafrancia Festival, attracting thousands of devotees. Camarines Sur is also a hub for water sports, with the Camsur Watersports Complex being a top wakeboarding destination. The province’s name originates from the Spanish word for “small huts,” reflecting its historical significance, and its people speak various dialects of Bicolano.
